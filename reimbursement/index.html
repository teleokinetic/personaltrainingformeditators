Where it fits

Use a budget that already exists

Companies rarely have a “personal training” line item — but most set money aside for employee wellbeing and growth. Training with me commonly fits under:

  • Wellness or fitness stipend — the most direct fit. This is personal training: strength, mobility, and a sustainable exercise practice.
  • Learning & development — the aim here is learning to fish. You leave with a durable skill — training yourself — not a routine you rent.
  • Coaching or professional development — one-on-one coaching that builds the capacity underneath your work: energy, focus, stress regulation.

If your company runs its stipend through a platform (Forma, Compt, Benepass, and the like), you may not need to ask anyone — just submit the receipt.

Good to know

Common questions

Which budget should I name?
Whichever your company already has. Wellness and fitness stipends are the most common route; learning & development, professional development, and coaching budgets also work. Use the name your company uses.
Can my company pay you directly?
Yes. I can invoice a manager, HR partner, or finance contact directly — no reimbursement round-trip needed.
What documentation do I get?
An itemized receipt or invoice: service description, session dates, amount, and my business details. If your finance team has a specific form or format, I'm happy to work with it.
Will my employer see what we work on?
No. Your company gets billing paperwork, not session notes. What you share about your training is entirely up to you.
Can I use an HSA or FSA?
Usually not. Personal training generally isn't HSA/FSA-eligible without a doctor's letter of medical necessity. The budgets above are the more reliable route.
What if my company says no?
Ask me about scholarships — I offer reduced-rate sessions for students, academics, non-profit staff, and people between jobs. Tell me about your situation and we'll work it out. Details under Cost & coverage.
