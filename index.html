01 · Why it matters

On the cushion, and off the cushion

Many meditators are far more at home in the mind than in the body. When the body becomes a place you can inhabit in all its joy and richness, your practice opens in two directions.

Feel good while you sit

  • Drop in faster — embodiment makes it easier to feel steady and settled
  • Stay comfortable longer — find an easy seat and meet discomfort skillfully
  • More to practice with — opening the body allows sensory experience to be vivid and available

Feel good while you move

  • Actually enjoy exertion — learn to experience movement as delightful
  • Extend practice off the cushion — equanimity in motion is less brittle
  • Functional strength & mobility — tailored to your body and life

02 · Who it's for

Is this you?

People come to this work from a few different places. See if one of these sounds like you.

“I think exercise is good for me — but I've never enjoyed it.”

Exercise has felt like a grind: forceful, joyless, something you should do. But you know from meditation that your relationship to an experience can change. Maybe it can change here too.

“I already move — but it feels cut off from my practice.”

Exercise is a habit you value, but you sense it could be more. You want the clarity you find sitting to come with you when you run, lift, or play on the floor with your kid.

“I'm spacious on the cushion — but it vanishes the moment things get dynamic.”

You're chill on the cushion. But ask you to juggle three balls — or lift, or run, or learn a dance step — and the spaciousness can evaporate. Where does it go when you start lifting weights? This work is about closing that gap.

03 · What we do together

What we actually do

The aim is a changed relationship to movement: from intimidated to autonomous, from a grind to something alive and life-supporting. We use whatever will help you most — strength training, mobility, health metrics and biomarkers, as well as somatics, standing meditation, and posture work.

From mind to body

You might have forty ways to work with a thought and ~none for how to work with your body. We practice attuning to space, sensation, and pleasure while moving. And make sure it transfers to what matters most, whether that’s lifting, running, or playing on the carpet with your toddler.

Games, tasks, improvisation

A testing ground for playfulness, and for expanding what even counts as “exercise.”

Movement quality and posture

Real-time feedback for moving with less risk and more elegance; and support to make sitting and standing practice more enjoyable.

Learning to fish

You're not a passive participant. The aim is always your own agency: to assess, adapt, and author your own practice over time.

I'm not here to break you, or write you off if you're not “tough enough.” The wider the base, the higher you can build — with most clients, I start from where enjoyment is already available, and help grow practice from there.

A typical session

Here are three session outlines, anonymized:

  • 0–10Check-in

C · Bridging strength and meditation

Already trains regularly, but it feels walled off from his meditation.

  • 0–10Check-in
  • 10–35Strength work — calibrating load, refining form
  • 35–60A guided practice to bring meditation into his runs

Each session shares the same through-line: movement as an extension of practice.

07 · Your trainer

Meet Tanner

Certified personal trainer since 2022. I was on track for a doctorate in physical therapy but left — I didn't want to spend the rest of my life handing people exercise printouts in a sterile clinic that I knew they'd never follow. My way into movement was elite athletics as a kid, but theatre school gave me my first adult taste of movement as joy, expression, and play. Since then I've worked normies and athletes, and one-on-one to help people out of chronic pain. These days I work mostly with meditators — people who already know transformation is possible, and want to extend that possibility into their bodies.

For those who love a list of modalities, here are some of my biggest influences: mainstream exercise science (TM), Feldenkrais, Alexander, Fighting Monkey Practice, Ido Portal, Vajrayana, dance. I've spent most of the last ten years immersed in these modalities; it's been a joy to shift my focus to teaching them.

08 · Good to know

Frequently asked

Is this remote?
Yes — over video. You'll need a bit of space and a camera.
How often would we meet?
We set a cadence that fits your life. The usual standard is at least one session per week. You commit to three months of work at the outset, so we have enough time to see real progress.
What will we actually do?
Depends entirely on you — strength, mobility, somatics, therapeutic work, general fitness, or a blend.
What does it cost?
$125 per session.
Do I need to be fit, flexible, or experienced?
No. You need willingness to be an active participant and to “learn to fish.”
